I finished my MSc dissertation on water quality monitoring and thought... that's it, it's done.

But the data kept nagging at me.

I'd spent months studying eutrophication — the process where ponds become overloaded with nutrients, triggering algal blooms, oxygen crashes, and fish kills. I'd built scoring models, reviewed stacks of literature, and collected real field measurements. And all of that was sitting in a PDF that almost nobody would ever read.

So I turned it into something people can actually use.

I built an interactive Water Quality Assessment Tool — a browser-based app where you enter 13 physicochemical parameters (dissolved oxygen, phosphate, nitrate, pH, temperature, and more) and instantly get:

→ A weighted Water Quality Score out of 100
→ An Eutrophication Risk rating (Low / Moderate / High)
→ A colour-coded breakdown of every parameter

No installation. No sign-up. Just open the link and go.

The thresholds are all grounded in peer-reviewed literature — Boyd (2019), Carlson's Trophic State Index, EPA guidelines, and data from my own dissertation research.

Try it here → [https://ybaah-biotech.github.io/yb-water-quality-tool]

If you've ever finished a dissertation or research project and felt like the work deserved a bigger audience — I'd really encourage you to build something from it. Even a weekend project can turn academic work into something tangible and shareable.

I built a Water Quality & Eutrophication Risk Assessment Tool as a mini-project after my MSc dissertation — here's what went into it.

The core is a weighted scoring model:
• 13 physicochemical parameters (DO, phosphate, nitrate, chlorophyll A, ammonia, pH, temperature, EC, TDS, PSU, ORP, TN)
• Each classified as Good / Moderate / Poor against peer-reviewed thresholds
• Weighted sum → Water Quality Score (0–100)
• Rule-based eutrophication risk engine on top (High / Moderate / Low)

The full deliverables:
✅ Interactive browser tool (vanilla HTML/CSS/JS — zero dependencies, works offline)
✅ Excel workbook with live WQS formulas and 3 pre-built scenarios
✅ Slide deck summarising the methodology and scoring model

A few deliberate design choices:
→ No frameworks — keeps it universally accessible and hostable anywhere
→ Thresholds sourced from Boyd (2019), Carlson TSI (1977), Elser et al. (2022), US EPA, WHO
→ Plain-English outputs so non-scientists can interpret results

Try the live tool → [https://ybaah-biotech.github.io/yb-water-quality-tool]

If you work in environmental monitoring, water management, or aquaculture and want to adapt the scoring model for your own parameters — feel free to reach out.
