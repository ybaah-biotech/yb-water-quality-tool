## Overview

This project implements a data-driven, threshold-based scoring methodology to
assess freshwater pond health. The approach translates physicochemical
measurements into a single interpretable score and an independent eutrophication
risk classification, making scientific data accessible to a general audience.

---

## Step 1 — Parameter Input

The user enters measured values for up to 13 physicochemical parameters:
Dissolved Oxygen (mg/L), Phosphate (PO₄³⁻), Nitrate (NO₃⁻), Chlorophyll A,
Ammonia (NH₃), Total Nitrogen, pH, Temperature, Electrical Conductivity (EC),
Total Dissolved Solids (TDS), Salinity (PSU), and ORP (mV).

Pressure (PSI) is recorded as an instrument reference field but excluded from
scoring, as it does not directly indicate pond ecological health.

---

## Step 2 — Threshold-Based Classification

Each parameter value is independently classified into one of three categories
using the threshold ranges defined in Section 4 (sourced from peer-reviewed
literature and MSc experimental data):

| Category | Score Assigned |
|----------|---------------|
| Good     | 100           |
| Moderate | 60            |
| Poor     | 20            |

The three-tier scoring scale (100/60/20) reflects meaningful ecological
distinction while remaining simple enough for non-specialist interpretation.

---

## Step 3 — Weighted Water Quality Score (WQS)

A weighted sum is applied to convert individual parameter scores into a single
composite index:

    WQS = Σ (Parameter Score × Weight)

Weights reflect the relative importance of each parameter to overall pond
health and eutrophication risk, as established by the environmental monitoring
literature. Parameters most directly linked to eutrophication and oxygen stress
(DO, Phosphate, Nitrate, Chlorophyll A) carry the highest weights. The weights
sum to 1.0, so the WQS is already normalised to a 0–100 scale.

The final score is classified as:

| Score    | Classification |
|----------|---------------|
| 80–100   | Excellent     |
| 60–80    | Good          |
| 40–60    | Moderate      |
| < 40     | Poor          |

---

## Step 4 — Eutrophication Risk Classification

In parallel with the WQS, a rule-based eutrophication risk model is applied.
This model focuses specifically on the parameters most predictive of
eutrophication events — phosphate, dissolved oxygen, and nitrate — and classifies
the pond into one of three risk categories:

| Risk Level    | Trigger Conditions                                          |
|---------------|-------------------------------------------------------------|
| High Risk     | Phosphate = Poor AND Dissolved Oxygen = Moderate or Poor    |
| Moderate Risk | Phosphate = Moderate OR Nitrate = Moderate or Poor          |
| Low Risk      | Phosphate = Good AND Nitrate = Good AND Dissolved Oxygen = Good |

Rules are evaluated in priority order (High → Moderate → Low). This two-model
approach ensures that a pond can score reasonably on the WQS while still
flagging a critical nutrient-oxygen imbalance that the weighted average alone
might not highlight.

---

## Step 5 — Output & Visualisation

Results are presented via:
- An animated radial arc gauge displaying the WQS (0–100) with colour coding
- A prominent eutrophication risk badge (green / amber / red)
- A parameter-by-parameter breakdown with horizontal bars and classification
  badges, allowing the user to identify which parameters are driving the score
- Plain-English explanations for each risk level, written for a non-specialist audience

The tool runs entirely in the browser (HTML/CSS/JavaScript) with no server,
login, or installation required, making it accessible to the widest possible
audience.

---

## Limitations & Assumptions

- Parameter classifications use fixed categorical thresholds rather than
  continuous scoring curves; values near thresholds may not reflect the full
  gradient of ecological impact.
- The weighting scheme reflects literature consensus but may not perfectly
  reflect the specific ecology of all pond types.
- The tool does not account for temporal dynamics (e.g., diel oxygen swings,
  seasonal variation) — single-point measurements are assumed representative.
- Pressure (PSI) is excluded from the scoring model as it is primarily an
  instrument calibration reference rather than an ecological indicator.

## Tool Validation — Example Scenarios

Three scenarios were used to validate the scoring engine:

| Scenario        | Key Values                                    | WQS | Classification | Eutrophication Risk |
|-----------------|-----------------------------------------------|-----|----------------|---------------------|
| Healthy Pond    | DO 9.2 mg/L, PO₄ 0.01 mg/L, NO₃ 4 mg/L      | 96  | Excellent      | Low                 |
| Moderate Stress | DO 6.1 mg/L, PO₄ 0.06 mg/L, NO₃ 14 mg/L     | 64  | Good           | Moderate            |
| Eutrophic Pond  | DO 3.2 mg/L, PO₄ 0.18 mg/L, NO₃ 28 mg/L     | 26  | Poor           | High                |

All three scenarios produced expected WQS values and risk classifications,
confirming the scoring and risk engines are functioning correctly.

## Key Findings

- The tool successfully translates complex physicochemical data into an
  accessible 0–100 score and a plain-English risk rating.
- Phosphate and Dissolved Oxygen are the dominant drivers of both the WQS
  (combined weight: 40%) and the eutrophication risk classification.
- The two-model approach (WQS + independent risk engine) captures scenarios
  where nutrient-oxygen imbalance is critical but may be masked by a moderate
  composite score.
- The browser-based format with no dependencies makes the tool deployable and
  accessible without technical expertise.
